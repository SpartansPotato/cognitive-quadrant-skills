# Originality and Prior Art

## Scope of the originality claim

This project does not claim invention of the four-part known–unknown framework. Its originality claim is limited to the applied workflow, local adaptation, package design, evidence rules, and deliverable system implemented in this repository.

## Related conceptual traditions

The project is informed by or adjacent to:

- Known–Unknown Matrix / Rumsfeld Matrix;
- metacognition and reflective learning;
- uncertainty and risk management;
- tacit-knowledge elicitation;
- double-loop learning;
- abductive and counterfactual exploration;
- evidence-informed decision-making.

These traditions are broader than, and predate, this repository.

## Related public Agent Skills and repositories

The following public projects are relevant examples rather than an exhaustive global search:

### exploring-knowledge-quadrants

Repository: https://github.com/William-Yeh/exploring-knowledge-quadrants

Similarity:

- uses the same four known–unknown quadrants;
- generates a quadrant-based knowledge map;
- includes exploration and web-enabled modes.

Differences in this repository:

- maps the quadrants to Express–Ask–Iterate–Explore as a user-facing action cycle;
- prioritizes ordinary Chinese users and teachers;
- includes WorkBuddy-oriented domestic-model and network-fallback rules;
- emphasizes multi-turn cognitive revision and concrete action design;
- includes editable Word, PNG, and spreadsheet deliverables;
- provides a dedicated teacher-research specialization.

### finding-unknowns-skills

Repository: https://github.com/bozhouDev/finding-unknowns-skills

Similarity:

- focuses on surfacing hidden requirements, tacit information, and unknown risks;
- encourages clarification before implementation.

Differences in this repository:

- focuses on general cognition and teacher research rather than software-requirement discovery;
- uses a four-stage cognitive workflow and document deliverables;
- includes knowledge-boundary expansion, theory/evidence retrieval, and research-question formation.

### Academic research skill collections

Examples:

- https://github.com/Imbad0202/academic-research-skills
- https://github.com/Imbad0202/academic-research-skills-codex

Similarity:

- literature research, research-question refinement, evidence checking, and academic workflows.

Differences in this repository:

- starts from the teacher's lived practice and tacit experience;
- treats cognitive diagnosis as the entry point rather than paper production;
- is designed for non-technical teachers using Chinese domestic-model environments;
- requires innovation ideas to state verification paths, evidence status, and risks.

## Practical originality summary

The strongest differentiated elements are:

1. Express–Ask–Iterate–Explore as a continuous cognitive contract;
2. a two-Skill family linking general cognition and teacher research;
3. China-ready WorkBuddy deployment with no mandatory external API configuration;
4. explicit three-level degradation when network or tools are unavailable;
5. user-facing editable office-document deliverables;
6. teaching-practice-to-research transformation for vocational and higher-education teachers.

## Limitations of this review

This comparison is not a legal novelty search, copyright opinion, patent search, or exhaustive search of private repositories and unindexed Skill marketplaces. Similar unpublished or private work may exist.
