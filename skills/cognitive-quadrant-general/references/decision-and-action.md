# Decision and action design

For decisions, clarify:

1. the outcome the user actually values;
2. non-negotiable constraints;
3. decision criteria and their relative importance;
4. known facts versus forecasts;
5. opportunity cost;
6. reversibility;
7. short-term, long-term, and second-order effects;
8. the information with the highest value of learning.

Do not collapse the result into “choose A” or “choose B” too early. When possible, design a small experiment, conversation, prototype, shadowing period, trial, or time-boxed commitment that produces evidence before an irreversible choice.
