# Evidence and search

## Search only when it matters

Use external research when the task depends on current information, professional knowledge, competing viewpoints, explicit verification, significant time or money, or high-stakes accuracy.

## Source preference

1. user-provided primary material;
2. official government, regulator, standard, or institutional source;
3. original research and peer-reviewed synthesis;
4. university or recognized research organization;
5. industry association or official company report;
6. reputable journalism for recent events;
7. expert opinion, preprint, forum, or weak signal as clearly labelled supplementary material.

## Evidence labels

- verified fact;
- source-backed interpretation;
- inference;
- hypothesis;
- unresolved uncertainty.

Seek counterevidence and alternative explanations. Explain why each important source matters. Do not use a list of links as a substitute for synthesis.
