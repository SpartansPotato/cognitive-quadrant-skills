# Innovation design and verification

Do not generate novelty by combining fashionable terms. A credible innovation direction should arise from at least one of these:

- conflicting findings;
- an under-studied population or context;
- a theory not yet applied appropriately to the setting;
- an unexplained relationship or mechanism;
- a practical intervention without mechanism evidence;
- a new technology that changes prior assumptions;
- an overlooked stakeholder;
- a missing boundary condition;
- a transferable method from another field.

For every innovation direction, provide:

1. current basis;
2. unresolved gap;
3. precise novelty claim;
4. research questions;
5. evidence and method;
6. feasibility and ethical risks;
7. status: well-supported, preliminary support, reasoned hypothesis, or high-risk exploration.

Never guarantee originality. Recommend a further literature and prior-art check before formal submission.
