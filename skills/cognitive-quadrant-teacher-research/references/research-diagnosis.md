# Research diagnosis

Check whether the proposed study has:

- a real and observable problem rather than only a topical theme;
- a defined population, setting, and boundary;
- constructs that are distinguishable and measurable or analyzable;
- evidence that the problem exists;
- a reason existing practice or research is insufficient;
- a plausible mechanism;
- available data and ethical access;
- a method aligned with the research question;
- a contribution that is more than a new label;
- a feasible scope for the user's time and resources.

Common reframing moves:

- effect → mechanism;
- technology → human–technology interaction;
- final product → learning or work process;
- average effect → differences between learners or contexts;
- positive outcome → trade-offs and unintended effects;
- isolated classroom → enterprise or professional standards;
- one intervention → implementation conditions and boundary cases.
